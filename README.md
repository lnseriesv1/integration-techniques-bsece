## 🎯 Overview

**Target Audience:** Bachelor of Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering (BSECE) students with complete beginner level knowledge

**What You'll Learn:** Master 5 essential integration techniques to solve complex calculus problems that form the foundation for circuit analysis, signal processing, and control systems in electrical engineering.

**Duration:** 4-6 weeks (40-50 hours total)  
**Effort:** 8-10 hours per week  
**Prerequisites:** Basic calculus concepts (limits, derivatives, antiderivatives)

## 🎓 Learning Outcomes

After completing this module, you will be able to:

✅ **Understand** why different integration techniques exist and when to use them  
✅ **Apply** u-substitution to simplify complex integrals  
✅ **Use** integration by parts effectively (including the tabular method)  
✅ **Evaluate** trigonometric integrals and use trigonometric substitutions  
✅ **Decompose** rational functions using partial fractions  
✅ **Choose** the right technique for any integral (decision tree strategy)  
✅ **Solve** real BSECE problems: circuit analysis, signal processing, Laplace transforms  
✅ **Verify** your answers using digital tools

## 📖 Module Contents

### Chapter 1: [U-Substitution (Change of Variables)](./01-u-substitution/README.md)
**Intuition:** Changing how you look at a problem makes it easier to solve

- Why u-substitution works (the "chain rule in reverse")
- 4-step procedure (easy to follow)
- 8 worked examples (from simple to complex)
- Common mistakes and how to avoid them
- **15 practice problems** with full solutions
- Real-world application: Area under curves in circuits

### Chapter 2: [Integration by Parts](./02-integration-by-parts/README.md)
**Intuition:** Splitting a complicated product into manageable pieces

- Product rule in reverse ("integration by parts" formula)
- LIATE rule for choosing u and dv
- Tabular method for repeated integration by parts
- 7 worked examples
- **15 practice problems** with solutions
- Real-world application: Energy calculations in systems

### Chapter 3: [Trigonometric Integrals & Substitutions](./03-trigonometric-integrals/README.md)
**Intuition:** Using trig identities to simplify periodic functions

- Integrals of sin(x) and cos(x) powers
- Integrals with tangent and secant
- Trigonometric substitution techniques (x = a·sin(θ), etc.)
- 6 worked examples
- **15 practice problems** with solutions
- Real-world application: AC circuit analysis (sinusoidal signals)

### Chapter 4: [Partial Fractions Decomposition](./04-partial-fractions/README.md)
**Intuition:** Breaking complex fractions into simple pieces

- Why partial fractions work (reversing fraction addition)
- Distinct linear factors
- Repeated linear factors
- Irreducible quadratic factors
- 8 worked examples
- **15 practice problems** with solutions
- Real-world application: Laplace transform applications

### Chapter 5: [Special Techniques & Strategy](./05-special-techniques/README.md)
**Intuition:** Knowing when and how to combine multiple techniques

- Completing the square for tricky integrals
- Reduction formulas (recursive approach)
- Integrals with irrational functions
- **Decision tree flowchart** (choose your technique!)
- 10 mixed problems combining multiple techniques
- **20 advanced practice problems**
- Strategy guide: "Which technique should I use?"

### Chapter 6: [BSECE Engineering Applications](./06-applications-bsece/README.md)
**Intuition:** Why engineers actually need integration techniques

- **Circuit Analysis:** Calculating charge Q = ∫I dt, voltage relationships
- **Signal Processing:** RMS values, power calculations, frequency analysis
- **Control Systems:** Introduction to Laplace transforms
- **Mechanics:** Work, energy, and motion problems
- 12 real BSECE problem sets with complete solutions
- Philippines-relevant examples (local circuit components, etc.)

## 💡 Study Tips for Success

### ✅ Best Practices

1. **Don't memorize formulas** - understand WHY they work
2. **Work through all examples** - don't just read solutions
3. **Practice daily** - 1-2 hours beats weekend cramming
4. **Draw pictures** - visualize the transformation
5. **Check your work** - use Wolfram Alpha to verify
6. **Form study groups** - teach others to solidify learning
7. **Write neatly** - your notation matters in calculus

### ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Example | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Forgetting **du** in u-substitution | ∫2x·e^(x²)dx with u=x² | Always include the differential |
| Wrong sign in integration by parts | -∫v du calculation | Double-check your minus signs |
| Incomplete partial fractions | Missing factor types | Check for linear AND quadratic factors |
| Losing the **+C** | Final answer without constant | Indefinite integrals MUST have +C |
| Wrong trig substitution | Using sin when need tan | Match substitution to expression type |
